Liverpool return to Premier League action on Thursday night when they host Sheffield United. The Reds go into the fixture on the back of a 2-1 win over Brighton on Easter Sunday. Jurgen Klopp's side will hope to maintain their place at the league's summit when they face the Blades in the midweek fixture. Before then, Arsenal will host Luton Town and City will face Aston Villa at home, with both fixtures taking place on Wednesday night..

Liverpool will still have several players unavailable for the match against Sheffield United due to injury. However, Klopp will be hoping that the injury list is beginning to ease ahead of the busy run-in, where one player could make a return for the game against the Blades.
